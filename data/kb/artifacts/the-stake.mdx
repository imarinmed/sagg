# The Stake

## Summary
The Stake is the wooden stake used by Kiara's grandmother (previous Countess Natt och Dag) to kill Count Dracula. It is preserved as a family heirloom at the Natt och Dag estate, representing the family's pivotal role in vampire history.

## BST (Blod Svett Tårar - Canonical from Transcripts)

The Stake appears in Season 1 as a historical artifact with profound implications for both vampire biology and political history.

### Dracula's Death

Kiara's grandmother killed Count Dracula using this stake, establishing the Natt och Dag family's legendary status in vampire society. The act confirmed that despite their supernatural abilities, vampires remain vulnerable to traditional vampire-killing methods (wooden stake through the heart).

The killing occurred during or near the transition period when the Blood Pact was being established. Whether Dracula's death was related to the treaty negotiations, a separate family conflict, or a necessary step in ending the vampire-human war remains unexplored in Season 1.

### Family Heirloom

The Natt och Dag family preserved the stake as a treasured heirloom, displaying it prominently at their estate. This preservation suggests:

1. **Pride in the deed**: The family views Dracula's killing as a source of honor rather than shame
2. **Historical significance**: The act was momentous enough to memorialize across generations
3. **Vampire vulnerability**: Keeping the weapon serves as reminder that even the most powerful vampires can die
4. **End of an era**: The stake represents the old world of vampire violence giving way to the Pact's peace

### Source Episodes

- **s01e03**: Kiara mentions her grandmother killing Dracula with the stake during conversation at the estate

### Implications

The Stake's existence has several narrative implications:

**Vampire Mortality**: Despite their supernatural advantages (super strength, enhanced senses, immortality), vampires can be killed by relatively simple means. This vulnerability creates tension throughout the series.

**Natt och Dag Power**: The family that killed the most famous vampire in history gained immense prestige. This historical deed likely contributed to their continued wealth and status in modern Sweden.

**Matriarchal Legacy**: As part of the matriarchal inheritance, the stake passes to Kiara as youngest daughter. She is heir not just to wealth and title, but to her grandmother's warrior legacy.

## SST (Svett Sperma Tårar - Dark Adaptation)

In the SST reinterpretation, The Stake transforms from heroic relic to symbol of betrayal, political assassination, and the dark compromises that enabled the Blood Pact.

### The Assassination Hypothesis

**Dracula as Obstacle**: Count Dracula, the legendary vampire who represented the old ways of human domination and blood hunting, likely opposed the Blood Pact's limitations on vampire power. His death conveniently occurred as vampire aristocrats negotiated integration with human society.

**Political Killing**: The Countess Natt och Dag didn't slay a monster - she assassinated the leader of vampire traditionalists who refused to accept the treaty. The "heroic" narrative masks a political execution that cleared the path for Pact ratification.

**Aristocratic Betrayal**: The vampire nobles who benefited from the Pact (retaining wealth, estates, and status) betrayed their own kind. They killed Dracula and likely other old-world vampires who wouldn't submit to the new order. The stake represents this internal purge.

### The Matriarchal Dimension

**Power Consolidation**: The Countess killing Dracula served dual purposes: eliminating political opposition AND demonstrating that the matriarchal line possessed the ruthlessness to rule. The act was both assassination and succession ritual.

**Youngest Daughter Inheritance**: The matriarchal system (youngest daughter inherits) means Kiara is heir not just to property but to this legacy of killing to consolidate power. Her grandmother's deed suggests what may be required of future Countesses.

**Mother-Daughter Succession**: The stake passes through the female line, each generation's youngest daughter inheriting both the weapon and the implicit lesson: survival requires eliminating threats, even from within vampire society.

### Erotic and Taboo Elements

**Phallic Weapon**: The stake's penetrative violence carries obvious sexual symbolism. The ultimate vampire death comes through violent penetration, mirroring and subverting the vampire's penetrative bite. The weapon feminizes and destroys simultaneously.

**Grandmother as Executioner**: The image of the refined Countess (matriarch, aristocrat, mother) driving a stake through Dracula's heart subverts traditional gender roles. She wields phallic power to destroy male authority, ensuring matriarchal succession.

**Death as Decoration**: Displaying the murder weapon as trophy in the family estate is macabre. Visitors to Natt och Dag home see the instrument of Dracula's death presented as art object, normalizing violence and celebrating betrayal.

**Kiara's Inheritance**: As heir to the stake, Kiara inherits her grandmother's role as potential killer. The weapon awaits the next time a Natt och Dag Countess must eliminate a threat to the family's power.

**Penetration as Ultimate Act**: The stake through the heart inverts the vampire's bite. Where vampires penetrate with fangs to sustain themselves, the stake penetrates to destroy. Both acts are intimate, violent, and transformative.

### Ritual Implications

**Execution Rights**: The Natt och Dag family may possess traditional rights to execute vampires who violate the Pact. The stake serves as symbol of this judicial power, making the Countess both judge and executioner.

**Blood Sacrifice**: Dracula's death may have been ritual sacrifice necessary to seal the Blood Pact. His blood, shed by the stake, might have consecrated the treaty between vampires and humans. The weapon thus carries metaphysical significance beyond mere historical artifact.

**Initiation Rite**: Future Natt och Dag heiresses might be required to prove themselves worthy by wielding the stake in ceremonial execution. The weapon tests each generation's commitment to maintaining the Pact through violence if necessary.

### Divergences from BST

1. **Heroism vs. Assassination**: BST presents Dracula's killing as heroic; SST reveals it as political murder
2. **Family Pride vs. Shame**: BST shows the stake as honor; SST suggests it should be source of guilt over betrayal
3. **Preserved Relic vs. Active Threat**: BST treats stake as museum piece; SST implies it remains functional weapon for future use
4. **Vampire Unity vs. Internal Conflict**: BST assumes vampires cooperated for peace; SST exposes violent purges of dissenters

### Implications for Kiara's Arc

Kiara inherits not just property and title, but complicity in her grandmother's act. She is:

- **Heir to Assassin**: Her grandmother killed to consolidate power; Kiara may face similar choices
- **Symbol of Compromise**: The Natt och Dag line represents vampires who chose integration (and wealth) over tradition
- **Keeper of Weapon**: The stake remains in her possession, awaiting potential use
- **Betrayer's Bloodline**: Other vampires may view her family as traitors who killed their own kind for human approval

The stake's presence in Kiara's life foreshadows potential violence. If she must choose between her humanity (relationship with Alfred) and vampire tradition, she possesses the weapon to make that choice permanent.
