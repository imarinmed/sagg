## Overview

The social hierarchy at Skaraborg High fundamentally privileges physical performance and athletic achievement as the primary determinant of social status, popularity, and institutional power. This system creates a stratified social order in which athletic success translates directly into social prestige, access to exclusive circles, and structural advantages within school governance.

## BST: Original Series Structure

### Athletic Performance as Status

In the original series, athletic achievement serves as the explicit metric by which social status is measured and maintained at Skaraborg High. Sports performance is not merely one factor among many—it is *the* factor that determines popularity, peer respect, and institutional position.

**Key Evidence (s01e01, s01e02, s01e05):**
- Team athletes occupy the highest tiers of social prestige
- Sports trophies and championship records are prominently displayed as status markers
- School recognition ceremonies prioritize athletic achievement
- Peer culture explicitly values athletic accomplishment over academic performance

### Eric's Captaincy and Social Authority

Eric's position as team captain positions him at the apex of the school's social pyramid. His authority derives from his athletic performance and leadership on the field, which translates into social influence across the entire school community.

**Manifestations:**
- Access to exclusive social circles and events
- Influence over peer decisions and social direction
- Allocation of social resources and opportunities
- Recognition from teachers, administrators, and peers

### The Grades-Status Disconnect

Notably, academic performance does not correlate with social status in this hierarchy. Students with exceptional grades but limited athletic involvement occupy lower social positions than athletic peers with unremarkable academic records. This explicit disconnect demonstrates that the hierarchy is genuinely sports-centered rather than merit-based in a broader sense.

### Party Committee and Social Roles

Athletic achievement determines allocation of roles in school social planning and governance. Team members and athletes disproportionately hold positions on party committees and other social planning bodies, indicating that athletic status translates into structural institutional power.

### Vampire-Human Integration

The introduction of vampire students into Skaraborg High creates an interesting dynamic within the established hierarchy. Vampire athletes—leveraging both supernatural strength and human social positioning—occupy particularly elevated positions within the school's social order. Their integration demonstrates that the hierarchy accommodates supernatural advantage while maintaining its fundamental sports-based structure.

## SST: Dark Adaptation Interpretation

### Predatory Subtext in Physical Dominance

The Skaraborg High hierarchy's emphasis on physical performance and dominance becomes explicitly predatory in the dark adaptation. Athletic achievement functions as a transparent marker of predatory capability and sexual dominance. The hierarchy reflects a system in which physical control and the capacity to exert dominance determine access to resources and bodies.

### The Body as Currency

In this interpretation, the body transforms from a vehicle for athletic performance into a commodity and instrument of power. Physical attractiveness, athletic capability, and bodily strength become inseparable from sexual desirability and hierarchical positioning. Those at the top of the hierarchy gain implicit access based on their demonstrated physical dominance; those lower in the structure become objects available for use by those with greater physical power.

### Trophy Culture as Objectification

Trophies cease to represent abstract achievement and instead become visible representations of conquest and control. The display of trophies mirrors the display of subjugated bodies. The act of winning—taking possession of the trophy—parallels the claiming of bodies through dominance.

### Vampire Strength and Predation

The integration of vampire students into the hierarchy makes its predatory nature explicit and literal. Vampire physical dominance is actual predatory capability; the hierarchy that privileges their strength is a system that privileges predators and their ability to consume. Vampire athletes occupy elevated positions not because of merit but because their supernatural strength makes them apex predators in a system that rewards predatory capability.

### Coaches and Institutional Authority

Authority figures—coaches and administrators—who oversee the athletic hierarchy become facilitators and enforcers of the power-imbalance system. Their institutional position allows them to leverage the hierarchy's power dynamics for personal advantage and to shield predatory behavior through institutional authority.

### Consent Obscured by Hierarchy

The hierarchy creates a permutation in which consent becomes obscured and functionally impossible. Lower-positioned students face implicit and explicit pressure to provide access and compliance based on their subordinate position. Physical powerlessness becomes a vulnerability that higher-positioned students are implicitly entitled to exploit.

### Performance Compulsion

Students who lack athletic capability but seek to improve their social standing face pressure to undergo bodily transformation, training regimens, and physical exertion that places them at risk of injury and vulnerability. The system compels bodily risk and performance for social access.
