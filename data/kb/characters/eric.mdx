# Eric

## Summary

Team captain of the Skaraborg Bats and chair of the school party committee. Eric represents the athletic hierarchy at Skaraborg High and plays a pivotal role in the integration of vampire student Kiara Natt och Dag into the school's social world. His on-and-off relationship with Batgirls leader Elise and his evolving relationship with Kiara drive key narrative moments throughout Season 1.

## BST

### Description
Eric is introduced in Episode 1 as the team captain of the Skaraborg Bats, the school's sports team whose trophy cabinet determines social hierarchy. He is also the chair of the party committee, positioning him as a central figure in the school's social infrastructure. At Skaraborg High, popularity is based on sports performance and contributions to the trophy cabinet, not academic grades, making Eric a high-status student.

He is dating Elise, the leader of the Batgirls' good-side faction, though their relationship experiences on-and-off drama throughout the season.

In Episode 3, Eric becomes the victim of Kiara's triggering when his sweat in a sauna provokes her vampire bloodthirst, leading to a biting incident. This event becomes a turning point in the narrative, as Eric is hospitalized (Episode 6) and the incident threatens Kiara's integration into the school.

Despite this traumatic experience, Eric demonstrates remarkable character growth. In Episode 5, while drunk at a warehouse party, he dances with Kiara, creating tension with Elise. In Episode 7, he delivers a public speech about inclusion and diversity that forces the Principal to reverse the ban on vampires attending the masquerade, allowing Kiara to participate.

Eric's arc represents themes of forgiveness, allyship, and the complexities of vampire-human integration.

### Physical Appearance
Human male, high school student (approximately 16-17 years old). Athletic build consistent with his role as team captain. High social status within the sports-based hierarchy of Skaraborg High.

### Key Relationships
- **Elise**: On-and-off girlfriend, leader of Batgirls good-side faction. Their relationship experiences tension, particularly around Eric's interactions with Kiara.
- **Kiara Natt och Dag**: Victim of her triggering/biting incident in Episode 3; later becomes an ally and supporter, enabling her attendance at the masquerade.
- **Skaraborg Bats**: Team captain, responsible for the team's performance and the trophy cabinet that determines school social hierarchy.

## SST

### Description
The dark adaptation transforms Eric into an embodiment of athletic masculinity confronted by vampire seduction and the destabilization of his social power. His high status in the sports hierarchy becomes a vehicle for exploring the erotics of dominance, submission, and the vulnerability of the privileged when confronted with supernatural desire.

The triggering incident in Episode 3 is reimagined as an explicitly erotic encounter. The sauna setting—heat, sweat, exposed skin—becomes a charged space where Eric's physicality provokes Kiara's bloodthirst. The combination of blood and sweat that triggers vampires is reframed as a metaphor for the mingling of violence and desire. The bite itself becomes an act of predation that carries sexual undertones, exploring themes of consent, danger, and the thin line between violence and intimacy.

Eric's hospitalization in Episode 6 is reinterpreted through the lens of trauma and recovery, but with added layers of psychological processing—does he fear Kiara, or is he drawn to the intensity of the experience? The adaptation explores complex responses to predatory encounters, including the possibility of traumatic bonding or the eroticization of danger.

His drunken dance with Kiara at the warehouse party (Episode 5) becomes a moment of public desire, alcohol-lowered inhibitions, and the negotiation of consent in intoxicated states. The tension with Elise is reframed as sexual competition and possessiveness, with Elise's anger reflecting territorial claims over both Eric and her social position.

Eric's Episode 7 speech about inclusion and diversity is reimagined as an act of public submission to vampire authority—a recognition that his social power is contingent and that true allyship requires vulnerability and the relinquishment of control. The speech becomes a performance of redemption, where Eric publicly acknowledges his complicity in exclusion and submits to the authority of those he previously marginalized.
